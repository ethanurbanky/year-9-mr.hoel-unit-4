Coral Reef

bio

Coral reefs are large underwater structures composed of the skeletons of colonial marine invertebrates called coral.
The coral species that build reefs are known as hermatypic, or "hard," corals because they extract calcium carbonate
from seawater to create a hard, durable exoskeleton that protects their soft, sac-like bodies. Other species of corals that are not
involved in reef building are known as “soft” corals. These types of corals are flexible organisms
often resembling plants and trees and include species such as sea fans and sea whip
